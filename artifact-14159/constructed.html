CONSTRUCTED & UNATTRIBUTED SYSTEMS

Attempts to build what history could not provide

After sufficient exposure to historical systems, an uncomfortable realization emerges: every cipher inherits the psychology of its creator. Reuse, shortcuts, and assumptions appear regardless of era.

These pages document my attempts to design systems that avoid the failures I have cataloged elsewhere. They are included for completeness, not endorsement.

Initial justification (2019): To understand failure completely, one must attempt success.

System I: Non-Repeating Symbolic Substitution

This system replaces plaintext units with symbols drawn from a deliberately overcomplete set. Unlike standard substitution, mappings are stateful: the same plaintext unit does not always map to the same symbol.

The intention was to suppress frequency analysis entirely by eliminating stable correspondence. Symbol selection depends on position and prior output.

Outcome: Frequency suppressed. Structural patterns emerged instead.

System II: Layered Transposition with Deferred Keys

This design combines multiple transposition passes with keys revealed only after partial decoding. The reader must commit to an interpretation before receiving information that may invalidate it.

I believed this would discourage brute force by forcing human investment.

Failure mode: I became the bottleneck. I avoided decoding my own work.

System III: Semantic Drift Cipher

Rather than encode characters or words, this system encodes semantic distance. Plaintext is paraphrased repeatedly according to constrained rules until the original meaning is obscured.

Decoding requires reversing interpretive choices rather than substitutions.

Critical flaw: Decoding accuracy depends on sharing my assumptions.

Artificial Scripts

Several scripts were developed in parallel with these systems. Their symbol sets were intentionally alien but internally consistent. Stroke order and visual similarity were tuned to invite false pattern recognition.

I have encountered readers who claimed partial understanding of these scripts. Their interpretations differed wildly and confidently.

Unsettling observation: The scripts succeeded as projection surfaces.

Self-Cryptanalysis

I subjected each constructed system to the same scrutiny applied elsewhere in this archive. Where no formal break emerged, I examined my own behavior.

Patterns appeared. Avoidance. Rationalization. Emotional attachment to elegance. These were not properties of the cipher.

Personal admission: I trusted my own constructions longer than warranted.

Abandonment

None of these systems are in active use. Not because they were broken, but because they demanded qualities from their users that I do not believe persist outside controlled environments.

The desire to invent an unbreakable system now strikes me as naïve. History does not lack cleverness. It lacks restraint.

Final assessment: Constructed systems reveal more about their creator than their security.
